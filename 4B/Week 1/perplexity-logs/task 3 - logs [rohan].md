Because “audience size” doesn’t equal “messages sent,” we model **monthly engagement rates**:

- **SMS cadence**: 1 SMS/month per contact (high-signal reminders only)
- **Inbound SMS**: assume inbound = 20% of outbound SMS volume (Q&A and replies)

- **Average SMS length**: 1 segment per message (conservative; longer messages cost more)

#### 5.5 HighLevel estimated variable costs at 1K / 10K / 100K contacts (US pricing baseline)
Let:
- \(N\) = contacts
- Emails/month = \(4N\)
- Outbound SMS segments/month = \(1N\)
- Inbound SMS segments/month = \(0.2N\)

Then:

- **Email cost** = \((4N / 1000) \times 0.675\)
- **SMS cost (excluding carrier passthrough fees)** = \((1N + 0.2N) \times 0.00747 = 1.2N \times 0.00747\)

Now plug in:

**A) 1K contacts**
- Emails: 4,000 → \(4 \times 0.675 = \$2.70\)
- SMS segments: 1,200 → \(1200 \times 0.00747 \approx \$8.96\)
- **Estimated variable subtotal** ≈ **$11.66/mo** (+ phone numbers, A2P monthly fee(s), carrier fees if applicable)

**B) 10K contacts**
- Emails: 40,000 → \(40 \times 0.675 = \$27.00\)
- SMS segments: 12,000 → \(12000 \times 0.00747 \approx \$89.64\)
- **Estimated variable subtotal** ≈ **$116.64/mo** (+ add-ons/fees)

**C) 100K contacts**
- Emails: 400,000 → \(400 \times 0.675 = \$270.00\)
- SMS segments: 120,000 → \(120000 \times 0.00747 \approx \$896.40\)
- **Estimated variable subtotal** ≈ **$1,166.40/mo** (+ add-ons/fees)

### Impact of **250K contacts** on tool fit (JKYog)

#### **GoHighLevel (GHL)**
- **Storage/scale**: GHL advertises **unlimited contacts & users** on its plans, so 250K contacts is structurally feasible. [1]
- **Cost reality**: costs are dominated by **sending volume** (wallet model), not storing contacts. LC Email is **$0.675 / 1,000 emails**. LC Phone SMS is **$0.00747/segment** inbound and outbound (plus A2P + carrier passthrough fees). [2][3]

**Illustrative monthly comms cost (GHL) — choose your own engagement rate**
Assumptions (same as earlier): **4 emails/contact/month**, **1 SMS/contact/month**, inbound SMS = **20%** of outbound, avg **1 segment**.
- If you message **100% of 250K** monthly:
  - Email: \(250K \times 4 = 1,000,000\) → **$675/mo** [2]
  - SMS segments: \(250K \times 1.2 = 300,000\) → **$2,241/mo** (before carrier fees) [3]
  - **Variable subtotal ≈ $2,916/mo** + base plan ($297 or $497) + A2P monthly fees + carrier passthrough fees. [1][3]
- If you message only **10% (25K)** monthly (more realistic for segmented journeys):
  - Email ≈ **$67.50/mo**
  - SMS ≈ **$224.10/mo**
  - **Variable subtotal ≈ $291.60/mo** (+ base plan + fees). [1][3]

**Conclusion**: For 250K, GHL is still the best “marketing OS,” but you must run **segmented journeys** (don’t broadcast to all 250K frequently) to control SMS costs and compliance risk. [1][2][3]

#### **Freshworks Freshmarketer**
- Freshmarketer differentiates **stored contacts (unlimited)** vs **marketing contacts**: anyone who receives **email/SMS/WhatsApp** in a given month consumes a “marketing contact” unit; limits reset monthly; add-ons are sold in **5,000** increments. [4]
- That means: if you try to actively message all **250K** in a month, you’d need **250K marketing contacts** provisioned (i.e., a very large add-on footprint), and WhatsApp also carries a **20% markup** from Freshworks on Meta-billed costs. [4][5]

**Conclusion**: Freshmarketer can work if you only activate a **small monthly subset** of the 250K (e.g., 10–30K engaged contacts/month). If your strategy requires broad monthly outreach to most of the list, Freshmarketer’s **marketing-contact gating** becomes a major constraint. [4][5]

#### **Podium**
- Podium pricing is **quote-based** and phones pricing depends on “units”; at 250K contacts you’re effectively in **enterprise procurement + negotiated limits** territory. [6]
- Podium is strongest for **inbox/phones/reviews/payments**, but it’s not designed as a funnel-first lifecycle automation OS the way GHL is. [6]

**Conclusion**: At 250K, Podium is best kept for **operational comms + phones + reviews** if it’s already embedded, while GHL becomes the system for **funnels + journeys + segmentation**.
